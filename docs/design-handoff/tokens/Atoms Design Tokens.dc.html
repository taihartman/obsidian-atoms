Color — semantic accents, never decorative

Every accent means one thing. In the plugin, tokens resolve through Obsidian theme variables first (so user themes win), with the iOS-dark value as fallback. Max one hero accent per screen; chips carry the rest.

New chip categories: possible, but rationed. A chip type must earn its color: it needs a stable classifier output (like person/work), not a one-off tag. Mint from Obsidian’s remaining named accents so themes still win — next in line: var(--color-yellow, #ffd60a) (e.g. place), var(--color-pink, #ff375f) (e.g. health). Same recipe as existing chips: 12–16% soft fill, full-strength text, radius 6. Hard caps: max 2 chips per row, ≤ 5 chip categories total app-wide — past that, new categories render as the neutral chip (#2c2c2e fill, secondary text, like “Relates”) until one is retired. Never reuse blue (action), green (done), or red (error) for a category.
Soft-fill recipe (narrowed in v2): color-mix(in srgb, ACCENT 9–18%, transparent) fills apply to typed chips and transient status cards (waiting, progress, done, error) only. Hero and claim cards are flat grouped surfaces with neutral hairlines: no gradients, no accent-tinted cards, no accent borders. Purple never fills anything.
Light theme rules. Obsidian vars carry surfaces and text automatically (bg #f2f2f7 · card #fff · sep rgba(60,60,67,.29) · label #000 / 60% / 33%). Two rules the vars do NOT give you: chip and status fills mix into the card surface, not transparent (color-mix(in srgb, ACCENT 12–16%, var(--background-secondary))); and accent TEXT darkens for contrast on light (color-mix(in srgb, ACCENT 75%, black)) — raw #ff9f0a or #64d2ff on white fails WCAG. Purple text likewise darkens toward #8944ab.

Type — one family, tight tracking, weight does the work

System stack only (-apple-system … system-ui) — the plugin lives inside Obsidian and must feel native to the OS. Negative letter-spacing scales with size. No uppercase except eyebrows and section labels.

Spacing
Base grid 2px, working steps 4 · 6 · 8 · 10 · 12 · 14 · 16. Screen gutter 16. Card padding 14–18. Card-to-card 14–16. Section label to content 8. Nothing above 24 inside the phone frame — density is part of the trust.
Touch & motion
Hit targets ≥ 44px (buttons 44–46). Interactive text ≥ 60% label opacity. Press state: scale(0.98) + opacity 0.92, 120–150ms ease. No hover-dependent affordances. Progress bars 6px, 3px radius. No confetti, no bounce, ever.
Truncation & width
Hero claim body clamps at 4 lines (ellipsis; Open shows the rest). Pair-view claims clamp at 8 lines. Library titles: 2 lines. Citator peer titles: 1 line, ellipsis. Chips: max 45% of row width, ellipsis. Serif quotes never exceed 65ch; on desktop the content column caps at 560px centered.

Usage notes
One hero per screen. Mind-change > on-this-day > connected > quiet. Everything else is a plain group or a status card.
Chips are typed, not tags. Person = orange, work/media = cyan, max 2 per row. Belief history is typographic purple, never a chip fill. A chip color used outside its meaning is a bug.
Connector (pair view): small-caps purple text on a hairline names the hard edge (revised by / contradicted by) between exactly two flat claim cards. Never a timeline.
Copy: plain punctuation. No em dashes in app-authored copy (periods and colons); the ban never touches user capture text, which is sacred and verbatim. Quotation marks around claims are chrome: rendered by the component outside the text node, never inserted into the capture. If a capture contains its own quotes, lists, or wikilinks, it renders as written inside the chrome quotes.
Buttons come in three grades: primary (solid tint), secondary (elevated fill, tint or normal text), quiet (transparent, secondary 60% text, full-width). Never more than one primary per card. Interactive text never drops below 60% label opacity; tertiary 32% is reserved for non-interactive meta.
In-plugin, always route through Obsidian vars (var(--interactive-accent) etc.) so light themes and user accents work; the hex values here are the dark fallbacks and the mock canon.
