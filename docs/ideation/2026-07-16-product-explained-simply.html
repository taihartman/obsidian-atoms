What Atoms is doing — and why some things feel empty

Last time I used product jargon (“cold open,” “graph,” “day‑7”). This page rewrites that in normal language, with pictures of what you actually see on your phone.

Atoms is three steps — not one app feature

Think of it like a kitchen, not a single button:

1

Dump (capture)

You already do this. iOS Shortcut → daily note. Messy bullets. No thinking required.

2

File (Process)

The plugin reads past days, asks AI what each bullet is, writes a clean atom note, and marks the bullet so it won’t run again. This is the “Process” button.

3

Surprise you later (For you)

Later, home shows one old atom again — “hey, remember this?” That’s the magic feeling. It only works after step 2 has been happening for a while.

Your instinct is right: if step 2 always needs a finger-press, it never feels like a brain. It feels like homework. Magic = step 2 mostly happens by itself.

What you said: “I don’t want to press Process”

That’s the product. When it works, you dump on your phone during the day, and overnight (or quietly when you open Obsidian) the plugin files everything. You open Atoms and just… see new atoms and maybe one old memory. No “go process the inbox.”

We already built a piece of this: auto-run (device-local, gated, silent). It may not feel “on” yet for you because keys, Sync, timing, or past-only rules get in the way — but the direction you want is exactly “file without asking me.”

What I meant by “cold open is weak”

Translation Cold open = the first time someone opens Atoms with almost nothing set up — not you after a day of building.

Imagine a friend installs the plugin. What do they see?

Atoms · first open
No atoms yet.
Set API key · dump some notes · Process
For you — nothing to show

That’s not a bug. It’s just that the magic needs three things lined up first: a key, some captures, and at least one Process (or auto-run). I called that a “weak cold open” meaning: a stranger wouldn’t feel magic in 30 seconds.

For you personally: this matters less. You’re already past setup. Your pain is more “I still have to think about Process,” not “I don’t know what this app is.”

Why “For you” is empty — and why that’s “by design”

Your Remote Vault today Roughly: a handful of atoms, all from today (or the same calendar day). Process worked. Markers worked. Library shows them.

For you is not “show me my newest notes.” Newest notes are already in the library under “Recently added.”

For you is: show me something I wouldn’t think to open — usually something older, or linked to what I just filed.

Library says

  • “Here are your latest filed notes”
  • Useful right after Process

For you says

  • “Remember this from another day?”
  • Needs history to work

How For you picks (simple version):

  1. On this day — same month/day last year or another year. (Needs atoms from past years/dates.)
  2. Connected — shares a person/show/link with something recent. (Needs a small web of links.)
  3. Quiet — older, not touched recently. (Needs atoms that aren’t all brand new.)
Why empty “by design”: all your real atoms are fresh and from the same day. There is no “older memory” yet, no anniversary, and almost no “quiet old note.” The code is working — it just has nothing eligible to resurface. Like a photo album app with only photos from this morning: “On this day” has nothing to show.

What I meant by “the graph”

Translation Not a chart. Not analytics. Graph = notes pointing at other notes with links — like a little web of your life.

When Process runs, the AI doesn’t just dump text into a folder. It creates a title, tags, and links such as:

  • this atom → [[Ning]] (a person)
  • this atom → [[My Hero Academia]] (a show)
  • this atom → another atom that revised an old thought

Those orange chips on home (people / work titles) are little windows into this web.

When I said “the graph is still thin”, I meant: you only have a few atoms, so the web is tiny — a few dots, not a constellation. “Connected” For‑you picks need shared links. With five same‑day notes, there’s almost nothing to connect across time.

You don’t need to “build a graph.” You just keep dumping. Process (or auto) files + links. Over weeks the web thickens by itself. That’s the product compounding — not a separate feature.

What “#1 — make day‑7 feel like a brain” meant

Sorry — that was the most jargon-y line. Plain version:

Goal After about a week of real use, opening Atoms should feel less like “inbox to clear” and more like “my life is accumulating somewhere smart.”

That requires either:

  • Time — keep capturing for a week+ so For you can find older / quieter notes, or
  • A temporary boost — seed a few older-looking atoms in the test/real vault so you can feel For you now (dogfood), without waiting for a real year of history.

I was not saying “ship a Day 7 feature.” I was saying: the product’s peak moment is delayed until history exists — so right now judging Atoms only by today’s 5 atoms under-sells what For you is for.

So what actually matters for you right now?

Strip everything else. Three plain ideas:

  1. Magic path: dump without thinking → filing happens without a Process tap (auto-run solid on phone) → home is for browsing + surprise, not “work.”
  2. Empty For you is normal this week: not broken. Wait for older atoms, or we can seed / soften rules so you can demo the surprise sooner.
  3. Links (“graph”) grow by using it: every filed atom that points at people/shows makes the next ones smarter and gives For you something to connect.
If we only improve one thing next for the “magic” feeling: make filing invisible (reliable auto-run / “just works” overnight), so you stop living on the Process button. For you gets better for free as the vault ages.

Money, strangers, cold open — those are “later, if this becomes a product for other people.” For your own brain, auto-file + time is 80% of the remaining magic.
